Our Story

Developed by the finest developers, Extra-Terrestrial Encroachers is a hit game made by three Junior Cert students using MakeCode Arcade, Microsofts free block coding software.
With a six week limit, these three students were able to make a game that incorporates groundbreaking movement, tremendous graphics, and a truly inspiring story. The development began how every triple A game developer thinks of a new and creative way to manufacture something new, unique and never-seen before. With a brainstorm. John Bernard Basanes, Maksymilian Surowiec and Hubert Kozłowski gathered together to think of a few quick ideas for their coding CBA. While everyone else was thinking of platformer games, and maze games, this legendary trio had a completely different idea. They were going to make a Space Shooter game, and nothing was going to stop them.

Development

They quickly began to scribble down their ideas on scrunched up scraps of paper. They knew that this was going to be a success, and nothing was going to stop them from achieving it. However, they soon began to realise that there was a massive flaw in their plan. Not everything would be able to be implemented into their game, as not everything would work well together.

The trio compromised, and each member of the development team chose three of their best ideas to be put into the game. They believed that this would be the best way to go.

John Bernard chose to add an interesting story to the game, chose the game to be in space, and for there to be a final boss in the game.

Maksymilian decided to add different types of enemies, such as asteroids, and varying types of aliens that would have to be fought. He also thought of implementing lives, and a score system to the game.

And finally, Hubert decided to go above and beyond, and make a custom, multipage website for the project. He thought of the menu screen, the artstyle for the game, and many other crucial elements of the game.

And so, the development began. John Bernard created a Google Docs document for the team to be able to track their progress. John Bernard and Maksymilian worked hand-in-hand to work at maximum efficiency, as Hubert managed to finish the website in record-breaking time. Everything was going to plan. As one student was busy, the other was bug testing and problem solving. It was as if they were performing a perfect symphony, working in harmony. It was a heavenly sight, some might say.

Final Thoughts

What we each thought of the game we managed to produce.

  • Maksymilian Surowiec:

    • Overall, I think that this project was a huge success. Not only did we create a fully functioning game, we also created a game that each of us had an influence over the development, and manufacturing of the game. Looking back, I wish we had a little teeny tiny bit more time, just to polish a few bits and pieces, but other than that, the game came out better than I ever would have imagined.
